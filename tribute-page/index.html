Sam Sepiol

Sam Sepiol (born November 19, 1988) is an American computer programmer and businessman widely known as a co-founder of tech start-up company Bleetz. After the company went public in 2014, Sepiol earned 17 billion dollars after the IPO. In 2014 he was named to the MIT Technology Review TR35 as one of the top 35 innovators in the world under the age of 35. For 2014, The Wall Street Journal gave him the 'Innovator of the Year Award' for technology.

Sepiol has released versions of the Bleetz app in 54 languages and, after opening office space in San Francisco last year, recently purchased a building in London for a British expansion. Though the number of employees in the company hovers around 500, Sepiol remains an active CEO.
